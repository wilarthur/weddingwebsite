Location

We are getting married at The Sancroft Barns, which is in Fressingfield, in North Suffolk.

Getting here

For those of you coming from Essex, you will need to follow directions towards Bury St Edmunds, until you see signs for Eye. The Sancroft Barns is located just before you reach Eye, in a rural village called Fressingfield.

Taxi companies

For people who want to get a taxi to a local place to stay, we reccomend booking them now. You can get taxis from Diss, Eye or Ipswich, but they must be booked in advance due to the location of the venue.

Places to stay

For places to stay, we reccomend looking at Airbnb as there are quite a few small cottages locally.

If you wish to stay in a hotel, the nearest hotels are in Diss for small local hotels or Stowmarket for large brand hotels.

Our story

Thank you so much for choosing to spend our special day with us. We absolutely cannot wait to celebrate our love with our closest friends and family.

We have had a pretty crazy journey so far and we thought we would take you on a trip down memory lane and tell you about our story leading up to becoming a married couple…

It all started in secondary school, when Erin, who spent most of her days dreaming of meeting someone, getting married and raising a family, started noticing this cute guy with floppy brown hair in the other half of the year. She always knew of him, but didn’t really think of him like that. One day she went on a school prefect trip and heard more about this boy she liked (who was not a prefect, but Erin liked the idea of a bad boy).

As she watched him in the playground every lunch time, she thought more about the boy until one day she plucked up the courage to message him. It started with a simple “hey x” to which he replied “hey :)” and as they say the rest is history…

They continued to message one another other constantly, sometimes staying up until 3am just getting to know each other, just 4 days later Wil decided (well was pretty much forced by Erin’s friends) to ask Erin to be his girlfriend.

They went on their first date, a trip to the movies and Wil stayed with Erin until her bus came. This meant he missed the only bus for himself, so his mum had to come and pick him up, in that moment Erin knew he was the kind of boy she wanted to be with.

They continued to meet up, but Erin was so nervous about the whole situation she refused kiss him, despite Wil trying on multiple occasions, including prom night where she rejected him in front of all their friends. Their first kiss finally came 3 months later, poor Wil!

They started college together and soon realised that they were so madly in love, as all teenagers think they are, that they wanted to spend every waking moment together. They would get the bus together, meet up between classes, go to lunch together, meet each other after college. They just couldn’t get enough of being with one another.

They spent the next year working on themselves, as teenagers they didn’t really know what they wanted in life but knew they wanted to face it together. They went to another college and ended up meeting some of their best friends, who are actually now in their wedding party and half of their guests… shout out to the “dimstitute” gang! They turned 18, went to way too many house parties, had a few too many drunken nights out and made a load of awesome memories, which they will cherish forever.

Both of them started working full time and now that Erin could drive it made meeting up a lot easier, this however meant they actually did spend every waking moment together. Wil became quite poorly, having been diagnosed with cancer, but Erin was with him every step of the way and was the one holding his hand when the news got broken to them.

Becoming poorly put life and love into perspective for them both and in that moment they realised life was so short, they couldn’t face not being together. At just 20 years old, they decided to put the small amount of savings they had into moving out together into their first home.

Soon after, Covid hit and they were faced with being a young couple with absolutely no money in the middle of a pandemic. Lots of at home date nights, panic shops, FaceTiming with friends and family, along with Erin creating a mini nightclub in the house for Wil’s 21st, meant they made it through just fine.

They welcomed their first addition to the family, their beautiful cat Lola. She was a funny little madam, who took a real shining to Wil, probably due to his calm, caring nature and a real disliking to Erin, probably because of her chaotic, loud energy.

They spent the few years falling even more madly in love, they absolutely adored each other and enjoyed spending time and making unforgettable memories with their wonderful families and friends. They hosted some awesome house parties, spent time outdoors, went on beautiful holidays and made even more wonderful friends.

One day Erin thought she was going for lunch with her mum, a pub lunch so nothing special, she decided to not bother having a shower or washing she hair. As they were driving, her mum told her that her dad, a mechanic, had left some tools at a local farmhouse and they needed to go and collect them for him. Erin was very confused when they got there, as she knocked on the door and the owner had no clue what they were talking about. She turned around and noticed Wil’s car and suddenly it started to sink in what was happening. She walked round to a small converted barn and saw it completely decorated, with Wil in a shirt waiting for her. She walked in and he gave a beautiful speech before getting down on one knee and asking the question she had always dreamed of him asking. They spent the day and evening talking about the planning that went into it, telling all their family and friends, eating a delicious homemade meal and slow dancing the night away to cheesy classics. They spent the next few days in a bubble, going to Cambridge to celebrate and taking as many opportunities as possible to show off that beautiful ring.

Wil told Erin all about how he told her parents. Erin had been very clear on the fact he didn’t have to ask permission, so he sat them down and said “I’m not asking you, I’m telling you that I am going to ask Erin to marry me” they were over the moon, he showed them the ring and told his family about it also.

Soon came the wedding planning, they went to different venues, chose their special date, saved their money harder than ever, ate lots of delicious food, had meetings with different vendors. In amongst all that, they knew they had to throw the most important house party of all, since they were known for it! They hosted their engagement party, 70 people in their 2 bed semi garden, on one of the hottest days of the year and they agreed, it was the best day of their lives so far. A small taster of what the wedding would be like and they honestly couldn’t thank their friends and family enough for all the love they received.

They decided to add to the madness by getting their puppy, Ned, less than a year before getting married. They knew they were crazy, but they enjoyed the chaos and they knew in the long run the stress would be worth it… Somehow the craziness didn’t stop there because less than 6 months before getting married, cat and dog in tow, they decided to move homes over Christmas. They were the sort of couple who thrived on everything being spontaneous and what better way to test that than to spend the whole of December sleeping on the sofa, planning a wedding, preparing for Christmas, training a puppy and trying to pack away everything they owned.

That brings us to present day, we are now living in our beautiful home, we have our wonderful pets, planning the wedding of our dreams and we can’t wait to celebrate with you all soon.

Thanks so much for reading our story, we hope it gives you a better insight into us and you can’t wait to celebrate our special day with us!
